The g‑Series

A Ladder of Scales — Not a Set of Constants

The g‑series is the structural backbone of AXLE and Principia Orthogona. It is not a list of fixed numbers or universal laws. Each g‑level marks a behavioral scale the system may enter as its internal dynamics evolve.

  • g⁶ — the cycle-scale: free, oscillatory, unconstrained. No theorem binds it; it is a regime, not a return.
  • g³³ — a soft equilibrium region: a moving target the system drifts around, with visible variance above and below.
  • g⁶⁴ — the phase-change threshold: the boundary where the system transitions into a new descriptive layer if equilibrium dynamics push it far enough.

These values are markers, not laws. They organize behavior without fixing it. In AXLE v6.1, all rigid invariants dissolve — there is no remaining theorem to prove. The system is free to move, drift, stabilize, destabilize, and reorganize across scales.
