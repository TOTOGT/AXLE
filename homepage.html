The g‑Series

A Ladder of Scales — Not a Set of Constants

The g‑series is the structural backbone of Principia Orthogona. It is not a list of cosmic numbers or fixed invariants. Each g-level marks a behavioral scale the system can enter, depending on its internal dynamics.

  • g⁶ — the cycle-scale: free, oscillatory, and unconstrained. No theorem binds it; it is a regime, not a return.
  • g³³ — a soft equilibrium region: a moving target the system drifts around, with visible variance above and below.
  • g⁶⁴ — the phase-change threshold: the boundary where the system transitions into a new descriptive layer.

These values are markers, not laws. They organize behavior without fixing it. In AXLE v6.1, all rigid invariants dissolve — there is nothing left to “prove” in the theorem sense. The system is free to move, drift, stabilize, destabilize, and reorganize across scales.
